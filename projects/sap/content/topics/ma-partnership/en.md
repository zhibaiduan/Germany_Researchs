# SAP's 5 Acquisitions and 10 Key Partnerships Over Two Years

**Research window:** 2024.06 – 2026.05  
**Core subjects:** 5 acquisitions + 10 partnership categories  
**Research objective:** Assess the intent and effectiveness of SAP's AI strategy  
**Thesis:** From ERP software vendor toward enterprise data and AI operating layer

---

This timeline is not simply a record of who SAP bought or partnered with. It traces how SAP is shifting enterprise software competition from "feature suite" to a battle for control over data, process, and AI execution entry points.

## Research Objectives

1. **Identify what gaps SAP is filling** — WalkMe addresses adoption, SmartRecruiters plugs the HR entry point, Reltio covers master data, Dremio fills the open data layer, Prior Labs adds structured-data AI.
2. **Distinguish real capabilities from ecosystem signals** — Databricks, Snowflake, and Google are closer to data-layer capabilities; Microsoft, AWS, NVIDIA, OpenAI, Mistral, Cohere, and Palantir each fill gaps in entry points, cloud, models, compute, sovereignty, and migration.
3. **Assess what this means for the market** — The key question is not "SAP expanded its product line again" but whether SAP can pull AI competition back to where it is strongest: business semantics, core processes, and enterprise data.

## Key Conclusions

SAP's main thread is not chasing general-purpose AI trends. It is assembling enterprise business semantics, trusted data, process execution, and user entry points into an operating layer that AI can call upon. Five conclusions frame the full analysis:

- **C1:** WalkMe fills the "bought the system but nobody uses it" gap — not the root cause of SAP's product complexity.
- **C2:** SmartRecruiters strengthens the SuccessFactors recruitment entry point — product-line reinforcement, not platform-level restructuring.
- **C3:** Reltio handles master data for customers, suppliers, and products — determining whether AI can trust the business facts it receives.
- **C4:** Dremio, Databricks, Snowflake, and Google collectively answer one question: how does non-SAP data enter SAP's business semantic system?
- **C5:** Prior Labs, OpenAI, Mistral, Cohere, NVIDIA, and Palantir respectively fill gaps in models, sovereignty, inference, and migration execution.

## Acquisition Timeline

### WalkMe (announced 2024.06, completed 2024.09)
Digital Adoption Platform, ~$1.5B. Addresses the problem of "systems bought but employees can't use them." DAP provides guided workflows, contextual help, task automation, and usage analytics. **Assessment:** Pragmatic. Especially suited to large European enterprises with strict processes, high training costs, and compliance pressure — but solves adoption and change management, not the root cause of SAP's product complexity.

### SmartRecruiters (announced 2025.08, completed 2025.09)
Talent acquisition SaaS, ~$1.5B. Fills the recruitment entry point gap in SuccessFactors, allowing HCM to engage earlier in the talent pipeline. **Assessment:** Clear product-line reinforcement. Does not change SAP's data foundation. The real test is integration: if boundaries with the existing Recruiting module are unclear, customers face duplicate purchases and roadmap confusion.

### Reltio (announced 2026.03, completed 2026.05)
Cloud-native master data management. Entity resolution, golden records, cross-system governance. **Assessment:** The most foundational move of this cycle — without trusted master data, the more AI automates, the higher the business risk.

### Dremio (announced 2026.05, expected Q3 completion)
Open lakehouse, Apache Iceberg. Makes BDC an open data layer rather than a closed warehouse. **Assessment:** High strategic value, but requires SAP to clearly define the division of responsibility among Databricks, Snowflake, and Dremio.

### Prior Labs (announced 2026.05, expected Q2/Q3 completion)
Tabular Foundation Models. Classification, regression, anomaly detection on structured enterprise data. **Assessment:** Largest long-term upside, but requires validation on interpretability, permissions, audit trails, stability, and industry fit before production use.

## Overall Assessment

SAP's moves in this cycle are not simply about adding SaaS products. The core is repackaging enterprise assets that are hardest to replicate — business processes, master data, transaction data, industry semantics, and execution entry points — as the control layer for the AI era.

- **Short-term easiest to deliver:** WalkMe, SmartRecruiters, Microsoft/Joule integration
- **Medium-term most critical:** Reltio + Dremio + Databricks/Snowflake/Google — determining whether BDC becomes a true enterprise data layer
- **Longest-term upside:** Prior Labs' Tabular Foundation Models
- **Primary risk:** Too many products, partnerships, and technical terms. Without deep integration into Business Suite, BDC, and Joule — and without clear architectural boundaries for customers — this strategy risks being perceived as complex, expensive, and hard to deploy
